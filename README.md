Sovereign Compute Standard (SCS‑1)
Version 1.0 — Published March 2026
Dynamic Artificial Intelligence Laboratories (DAIL)

Overview
The Sovereign Compute Standard (SCS‑1) defines the global requirements, architecture, governance model, and verification framework for compute environments that support artificial intelligence systems under conditions of sovereignty, integrity, provenance, and reproducibility. It establishes the foundational layer of the DAIL Standards Ecosystem and serves as the authoritative reference for institutions seeking to deploy trustworthy, independently verifiable AI systems.
SCS‑1 applies to classical, accelerated, distributed, edge, and quantum‑hybrid compute environments. It is technology‑neutral and implementation‑agnostic, focusing on the mandatory characteristics that sovereign compute systems shall satisfy.
For a detailed record of the origin and evolution of the standard, see the project’s historical overview in HISTORY.md.
Purpose
SCS‑1 exists to ensure that AI workloads:
• 	Execute independently of external influence
• 	Maintain complete, tamper‑evident provenance
• 	Operate with continuous integrity verification
• 	Are fully reproducible under equivalent conditions
• 	Remain accountable to a designated governance authority
• 	Support independent audit and verification
The standard provides a rigorous foundation for national laboratories, research institutions, critical infrastructure operators, and organizations responsible for AI governance and safety.

Relationship to the DAIL Standards Ecosystem
SCS‑1 is the foundational compute‑layer standard within the broader DAIL ecosystem. Additional standards build on SCS‑1, including:
• 	RIS‑1: Reproducible Intelligence Standard
Requirements for reproducible AI models, pipelines, and decision pathways.
• 	QAI‑1: Quantum‑AI Infrastructure Framework
Governance and architectural requirements for quantum‑AI hybrid systems.
• 	DKG‑1: DAIL Knowledge Graph Specification
Machine‑readable ontologies encoding SCS‑1 definitions, requirements, and governance structures.
Future standards will extend this ecosystem to cover safety, evaluation, deployment, and institutional governance.

Governance and Maintenance
SCS‑1 is maintained by the designated governance authority within Dynamic Artificial Intelligence Laboratories (DAIL). All proposed changes must:
• 	Be submitted through a documented review process
• 	Include justification, impact analysis, and draft text
• 	Undergo technical and governance review
• 	Receive approval before incorporation into a future version
Independent verification bodies may provide recommendations but do not modify the standard directly.
