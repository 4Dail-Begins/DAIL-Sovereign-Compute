Sovereign Compute Standard (SCS‑1)
Dynamic Artificial Intelligence Laboratories (DAIL)

Purpose of This Roadmap
This roadmap outlines the planned evolution of the Sovereign Compute Standard (SCS‑1). It provides institutions, implementers, and auditors with visibility into upcoming clarifications, enhancements, and major revisions. The roadmap is non‑normative and may evolve as new requirements, technologies, and governance needs emerge.
All changes follow the formal change‑control process defined in GOVERNANCE.md.

Versioning Overview
SCS‑1 uses semantic versioning:
• 	Major versions (X.0) introduce structural or normative changes.
• 	Minor versions (X.Y) introduce clarifications, non‑breaking improvements, or editorial updates.
This roadmap reflects both categories.

Planned Minor Versions
v1.1 — Clarifications and Expanded Guidance
Focus: Strengthening clarity, consistency, and implementability without altering normative requirements.
Planned updates:
• 	Expanded definitions in Section 3
• 	Additional examples in annexes
• 	Clarifications on trust boundaries and attestation workflows
• 	Improved guidance for hybrid classical‑quantum environments
• 	Editorial refinements for precision and consistency
• 	Additional diagrams in the architecture overview
Target: Near‑term release following community feedback and early institutional adoption.

v1.2 — Reproducibility and Provenance Enhancements
Focus: Deepening reproducibility and provenance guidance based on early implementation patterns.
Planned updates:
• 	Additional reproducibility scenarios
• 	Expanded provenance schemas aligned with DKG‑1
• 	Clarifications on controlled‑stochastic execution
• 	Additional guidance for distributed and federated environments
Target: Medium‑term release after v1.1 adoption.

Planned Major Versions
v2.0 — Structural Expansion and Cross‑Standard Integration
Focus: Incorporating lessons from early adopters and aligning SCS‑1 with the broader DAIL standards ecosystem.
Planned updates:
• 	Structural reorganization for improved readability
• 	Integration points with RIS‑1, QAI‑1, and DKG‑1
• 	Expanded normative requirements for hybrid classical‑quantum systems
• 	Updated governance and compliance frameworks
• 	New annexes for sector‑specific implementations (e.g., research, critical infrastructure, government)
This version introduces new normative content and may require institutions to update their implementations.

v3.0 — Federated Sovereignty and Multi‑Institution Governance
Focus: Supporting sovereign compute across multi‑institution ecosystems.
Planned updates:
• 	Normative requirements for federated sovereignty
• 	Cross‑institution provenance and attestation models
• 	Governance structures for multi‑party oversight
• 	Interoperability requirements for sovereign clusters
• 	Lifecycle management for distributed sovereign systems
This version reflects the long‑term evolution of sovereign compute as a national and global capability.

Long‑Term Research and Development Areas
The following areas are under active exploration and may inform future versions:
• 	Quantum‑secure provenance and attestation
• 	Automated compliance verification
• 	AI‑assisted reproducibility validation
• 	Sovereign compute for edge‑native and sensor‑rich environments
• 	Machine‑readable governance frameworks (DKG‑1 expansion)
• 	Integration with national and international standards bodies
These areas are not yet scheduled for specific versions but represent strategic priorities.

Community and Institutional Feedback
The roadmap evolves based on:
• 	Implementation feedback
• 	Governance reviews
• 	Security and integrity assessments
• 	Public commentary submitted through issues and proposals
• 	Cross‑standard alignment within the DAIL ecosystem
Institutions are encouraged to submit structured proposals using the templates provided in this repository.

Closing Notes
This roadmap provides visibility into the future direction of SCS‑1 while maintaining flexibility to adapt to emerging technologies and institutional needs. All updates will follow the formal governance process and will be documented in the versioned directories and CHANGELOG.
