CODE_OF_CONDUCT.md
Sovereign Compute Standard (SCS‑1)
Dynamic Artificial Intelligence Laboratories (DAIL)

Purpose
This Code of Conduct establishes the expectations for all contributors, reviewers, maintainers, and observers participating in discussions, proposals, or governance activities related to the Sovereign Compute Standard (SCS‑1). It ensures that the standards process remains professional, respectful, and aligned with the mission of sovereign, reproducible, and trustworthy AI infrastructure.
All participants are required to follow this Code of Conduct in all interactions within this repository and any associated communication channels.

Standards of Professional Conduct
Respectful Communication
Participants must engage with professionalism and courtesy. This includes:
• 	Using clear, constructive, and respectful language
• 	Focusing on technical merit, not personal attributes
• 	Avoiding sarcasm, hostility, or dismissive behavior
• 	Listening to and acknowledging differing viewpoints
Technical Rigor
Contributors must:
• 	Provide evidence‑based reasoning
• 	Support claims with references or analysis
• 	Avoid speculation presented as fact
• 	Ensure proposals are precise, unambiguous, and aligned with SCS‑1 principles
Integrity and Transparency
Participants must:
• 	Disclose conflicts of interest when relevant
• 	Avoid misrepresenting data, results, or institutional positions
• 	Refrain from submitting intentionally misleading or incomplete proposals

Unacceptable Behavior
The following behaviors are prohibited:
• 	Personal attacks, insults, or derogatory comments
• 	Harassment, intimidation, or threats
• 	Discriminatory language or conduct
• 	Knowingly submitting false, deceptive, or plagiarized content
• 	Attempting to bypass governance processes
• 	Public disclosure of security vulnerabilities without following SECURITY.md
• 	Disrupting discussions or undermining the standards process

Responsibilities of Maintainers
Maintainers (including the DAIL Standards Council and Standards Editors) are responsible for:
• 	Enforcing this Code of Conduct consistently and fairly
• 	Reviewing contributions based on merit and alignment with SCS‑1
• 	Providing clear, actionable feedback
• 	Removing or rejecting contributions that violate this Code
• 	Taking corrective action when necessary, including restricting participation
Maintainers must model the highest standards of professionalism.

Reporting Violations
Violations of this Code of Conduct may be reported confidentially to the DAIL Standards Council through the designated reporting channel. Reports should include:
• 	Description of the behavior
• 	Context and location (issue, pull request, discussion)
• 	Any supporting evidence
All reports will be reviewed promptly and handled with discretion.

Consequences of Violations
Depending on severity, consequences may include:
• 	Private or public warning
• 	Temporary or permanent removal from discussions
• 	Rejection of contributions
• 	Escalation to institutional leadership
• 	In severe cases, banning from participation in DAIL standards activities
The DAIL Standards Council has final authority over enforcement decisions.

Scope
This Code of Conduct applies to:
• 	All interactions within this repository
• 	All proposals, issues, pull requests, and discussions
• 	Any communication related to SCS‑1 governance or development
• 	Any official or semi‑official forums associated with DAIL standards

Amendments
Changes to this Code of Conduct follow the same governance and change‑control process defined in GOVERNANCE.md. Amendments must be approved by the DAIL Standards Council and recorded in the CHANGELOG.
