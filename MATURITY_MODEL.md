Sovereign Compute Standard (SCS‑1)
Dynamic Artificial Intelligence Laboratories (DAIL)

Purpose of This Model
This maturity model defines the six levels of institutional capability for implementing the Sovereign Compute Standard (SCS‑1). It provides a structured framework for assessing progress, identifying gaps, and planning long‑term modernization. The model is non‑normative and does not replace the requirements of SCS‑1.
Each level builds on the previous one and reflects increasing alignment with sovereign compute principles: sovereignty, integrity, provenance, reproducibility, and governance.

Level 0 — No Implementation
Institutions at this level have not begun implementing SCS‑1 and may lack awareness of sovereign compute concepts.
Characteristics include:
• 	No formal governance or authority for sovereign compute
• 	Limited or no provenance, attestation, or reproducibility controls
• 	Infrastructure dependencies that compromise sovereignty
• 	Fragmented or undocumented operational practices
This level represents a starting point, not a compliant posture.

Level 1 — Initial Awareness
Institutions recognize the need for sovereign compute and have begun preliminary exploration.
Characteristics include:
• 	Awareness of SCS‑1 principles and requirements
• 	Initial discussions about governance and trust boundaries
• 	Early identification of infrastructure and capability gaps
• 	Limited documentation of current-state architecture
This level indicates conceptual readiness but no operational capability.

Level 2 — Partial Implementation
Institutions have implemented some foundational elements of SCS‑1 but lack full coverage across all domains.
Characteristics include:
• 	Partial physical sovereignty over infrastructure
• 	Initial integrity controls (e.g., secure boot, basic isolation)
• 	Early provenance capture for select workloads
• 	Limited reproducibility mechanisms
• 	Governance roles identified but not fully operational
This level reflects meaningful progress but insufficient alignment for sovereign operation.

Level 3 — Operational Implementation
Institutions have implemented the core requirements of SCS‑1 and can operate sovereign compute environments for defined workloads.
Characteristics include:
• 	Physical sovereignty established for critical systems
• 	Integrity controls deployed across production environments
• 	Provenance and attestation integrated into workflows
• 	Reproducibility pipelines operational for key workloads
• 	Governance authority active and enforcing policies
• 	Documentation and audit trails maintained
This level represents functional, operational alignment with SCS‑1.

Level 4 — Verified Compliance
Institutions demonstrate full compliance with SCS‑1 across all domains and maintain continuous verification.
Characteristics include:
• 	Comprehensive sovereignty across all compute environments
• 	Integrity, provenance, and reproducibility controls validated through audits
• 	Attestation workflows fully integrated and independently verifiable
• 	Governance processes mature, documented, and consistently applied
• 	Continuous monitoring for non‑conformance
• 	Evidence‑based compliance records maintained
This level reflects robust, verifiable adherence to SCS‑1.

Level 5 — Continuous Sovereign Operation
Institutions operate sovereign compute as a sustained, strategic capability with continuous improvement and lifecycle governance.
Characteristics include:
• 	Sovereign compute integrated into institutional mission and strategy
• 	Continuous verification of integrity, provenance, and reproducibility
• 	Automated compliance and governance workflows
• 	Advanced provenance integration (e.g., DKG‑1 alignment)
• 	Lifecycle management for infrastructure, documentation, and governance
• 	Proactive identification and mitigation of risks
• 	Institutional culture centered on sovereignty and reproducibility
This level represents the highest standard of sovereign compute maturity.

Using the Maturity Model
Institutions should use this model to:
• 	Assess current capabilities
• 	Identify gaps across the five SCS‑1 domains
• 	Prioritize modernization efforts
• 	Support readiness reporting
• 	Guide long‑term strategic planning
The model aligns directly with the ASSESSMENT_CHECKLIST.md and READINESS_REPORT_TEMPLATE.md, enabling consistent evaluation and reporting.
